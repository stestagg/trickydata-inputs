real_world_url_fragment_looks_like_authority

Everything after `#` is a fragment and is not part of the authority or HTTP request target. UI truncation or display reordering can make the attacker-shaped text look significant even though the actual host remains `trusted.example`. RFC 3986 sections 3.5 and 7.6 discuss fragments and semantic attacks: https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3986.html#section-3.5 https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3986.html#section-7.6
