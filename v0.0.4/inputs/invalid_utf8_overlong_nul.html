invalid_utf8_overlong_nul

C0 80 is the non-shortest, two-byte representation historically used for U+0000 by Modified UTF-8. Standard UTF-8 requires NUL to be encoded as the single byte 00 and forbids both the 0xC0 starter and overlong encodings. Accepting this form can reintroduce NUL after validation. See RFC 3629's shortest-form requirement: https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc3629
