real_world_email_slash_equals_local_part

Slash and equals are both valid unquoted `atext` characters. This routing-shaped local-part is syntactically valid but often fails simplified regular expressions, URL-oriented escaping, and form-validation libraries. RFC 5322 section 3.2.3 includes slash and equals in `atext`, which RFC 5321 section 4.1.2 uses for SMTP atoms: https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc5322.html#section-3.2.3 https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc5321.html#section-4.1.2
