real_world_email_all_atext_symbols

This local-part contains every non-alphanumeric ASCII symbol allowed by the RFC 5322 `atext` production. Validators built around a small letters-digits-dot-hyphen character class often reject one or more of them, even though the unquoted dot-string is valid SMTP mailbox syntax. RFC 5322 section 3.2.3 defines `atext`, and RFC 5321 section 4.1.2 imports it: https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc5322.html#section-3.2.3 https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc5321.html#section-4.1.2
